# 01.2 — Brand & Design Direction

> **Note:** The authoritative visual system is in `01-context/1.3-design-system.md`. That doc has the final tokens, components, animations, and page templates. This doc covers brand positioning, voice, and design rationale. Read both.

## The core tension to resolve

Grandpa wants **mom-and-pop style that invites both B2B and B2C — professional but warm and inviting.**

That's a real design challenge. Most e-commerce templates are either cold and B2B-ish (think WebstaurantStore) or warm and B2C-cute (think Etsy / indie food brands). La Costa Gourmet needs to feel like a family business that still takes your café order seriously.

## Brand positioning statement

> **"Family-owned specialty beverage supply, serving cafés and home enthusiasts from Carlsbad, California since 2003. Every product made in the USA. The same quality the big guys use, shipped by someone who knows your name."**

## Reference brands to study (look at THESE, not generic e-commerce)

**Best direct analogs:**

- **[King Arthur Baking](https://kingarthurbaking.com)** — family-owned (employee-owned), professional enough for commercial bakers, warm enough for home bakers, real heritage without leaning on it too hard. **This is the north star.**
- **[Red Boat Fish Sauce](https://redboatfishsauce.com)** — smaller, family-made, but sells to real restaurants. Note the warmth of the founder's story and the credibility of the business.
- **[Penzeys Spices](https://www.penzeys.com)** — actually a bit too casual/chaotic, but note how they handle B2B + B2C on the same site.

**Anti-references (do NOT look like):**

- Amazon (too cold, too busy)
- WebstaurantStore (B2B sterile)
- Etsy sellers (too cute, no B2B credibility)
- Shopify default themes (too generic, no personality)
- Any site that uses the word "artisanal" more than once

## Visual direction

### Color palette

Warm, grounded, food-category-appropriate. **Not** purple, not neon, not black-and-white minimalism.

```
Primary:        #8B4513  (saddle brown — warm, cocoa-adjacent)
                OR
                #2D4A3E  (deep forest — "grounded family business")
                OR
                #7B3F00  (chocolate — ties to cocoa/chai products)

Background:     #FAF6F0  (warm off-white, paper-like)
                OR
                #F5EFE6  (cream)

Accent:         #D4A574  (warm tan, for CTAs and highlights)
                OR
                #C4441C  (terracotta, for sale / accent moments)

Text:           #2C2420  (warm black, never pure #000)
Muted text:     #6B5D54  (warm gray-brown)

Borders:        #E8DFD3  (soft cream border, never gray)
```

Claude Code should lock these into CSS variables (`--color-primary`, `--color-bg`, etc.) before writing any components so they're reusable.

### Typography

**Headings:** a warm serif with personality. Good options (in preference order):

1. **Fraunces** (Google Fonts) — modern serif with warmth, slight quirkiness
2. **DM Serif Display** — cleaner, more heritage-feeling
3. **Playfair Display** — safe, elegant, slightly overused

**Body:** a clean, readable sans-serif. Good options:

1. **Source Sans 3** — readable, neutral, warmer than Inter
2. **Work Sans** — friendly geometric
3. **Nunito** — slightly rounded, approachable

**AVOID:** Inter (too generic, too AI-slop), Roboto, Arial, any system font stack

### Layout principles

- **Generous whitespace** — the site should breathe. No walls of products 12-across.
- **Asymmetry OK** — headers don't need to be dead-center
- **Paper-like backgrounds** — subtle noise texture or cream color, never flat pure white
- **Real photography over illustration** — if we have to use stock, use very specific food photography (warm, shadowy, editorial) not clip-art
- **No carousels on homepage** — they don't work, everyone knows they don't work, don't use them
- **Footer is substantial** — not an afterthought. Includes address, phone, "since 2003," newsletter signup, category quick-links, trust signals (TrustWave, Authorize.net badges carry over from current site)

### Component details

- **Buttons:** warm accent color, slightly rounded (not pill, not sharp), hover state that darkens rather than changes color
- **Product cards:** photo on cream background, serif product title, sans-serif meta, clear price, "Add to Cart" button that looks like a button
- **Forms:** generous input padding, clear labels, never placeholder-only inputs
- **Category pages:** breadcrumbs always, filters on the left (not a modal)
- **Product detail pages:** photo left, info right, "About this product" tab, pack size / nutrition / ingredients clearly laid out

## Copy voice

### Guiding principles

- **First person sometimes.** "We've been doing this since 2003" not "La Costa Gourmet has been operating since 2003."
- **Specific over generic.** "Family-owned in Carlsbad, California" not "family-owned business."
- **Warm but not twee.** "Our favorite" is OK. "Handcrafted with love" is not.
- **Respect the buyer.** No "DEAL! LIMITED TIME!" shouty copy. They're adults buying chai mix.
- **Trust signals woven in.** "Since 2003" and "Made in the USA" appear naturally throughout, not just on the About page.

### Headlines grandpa wants to see on the site

- "Made in the USA" — every product
- "Family-owned since 2003"
- "Serving cafés and home enthusiasts from Carlsbad, California"
- "5-star service, best in class" — use this kind of language somewhere, maybe in testimonials area or About page

### About page (full section — grandpa's story)

Write the About page as a real page, not a corporate blurb. It should tell:

- When and why La Costa Gourmet started (2003)
- That it's family-owned
- That everything is made in the USA
- Grandpa's perspective on quality, service, customers
- Specific — name the city (Carlsbad), reference real brands they carry, mention the 22 years of doing this

**Claude Code should write a first draft, then leave placeholders where grandpa's actual story details go.** Don't invent facts.

### Product descriptions

BigCommerce currently has manufacturer-boilerplate descriptions. **v1 keeps these** (it's fine, most are informative). **v2 enhancement:** rewrite top 50 SKU descriptions in La Costa Gourmet voice — "We've been carrying this since [year]" kind of touches.

## B2B-without-breaking-warmth elements

These are the things that signal "you can trust us for your café supply" without going cold:

1. **"For Business" page in nav** — not buried in footer. Warm copy explaining volume pricing, bulk orders, cafes they serve
2. **"This is for my business" checkbox at checkout** — captures company name + tax ID field (tax-exempt reseller filing capability = v2)
3. **Volume pricing hint on product pages** — small line under the price: "Ordering 6+ cases? [Contact us for volume pricing.](/contact)"
4. **Line sheet downloads** — PDF product catalogs on the For Business page, downloadable per category. Grandpa can hand these out at trade shows.
5. **Trust badges** — TrustWave and Authorize.net Verified Merchant badges carry over (he has these on the current site). These matter for B2B credibility.
6. **Phone number prominent in header** — B2B buyers call. His number goes in the header right side, click-to-call on mobile.
7. **"Net 30 available for qualified businesses"** — footer line. Reality: he doesn't really do Net 30, but the language signals legitimacy. Remove if grandpa doesn't want this.

## What Claude Code should NOT do with design

- Don't use Tailwind's default `blue-500` / `gray-400` palette — override with the warm palette above
- Don't use Lucide icons in place of actual imagery — for food, photos > icons
- Don't make the hero a video — slow, distracting
- Don't use "AI" in any copy — grandpa's customers don't care and it cheapens the brand
- Don't build an infinite-scroll homepage — give it a clear end
- Don't add a chatbot widget — wrong vibe
- Don't use emojis in product listings or UI chrome — keep it dignified
